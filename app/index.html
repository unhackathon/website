A New Kind of Hackathon

New York City, NY

What is Unhackathon?


Unhackathon is the place for learning and experimenting. Whether you want to learn learn to code, experiment with a new programming language, or check out a new API, we will make sure there is an abundance of resources and mentorship to help you along the way. Most importantly, we want to make sure everyone is going home with something they're proud of.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where programmers, designers, and people of all kinds come together to build amazing projects within a single weekend.

Who can attend?

Unhackathon is open to any student who wants to spend a weekend building something great! Whether this is your first hackathon or your 15th, you’ll have opportunities to learn from our hackers and mentors, compete for awards, and have an epic time.

How big are teams?

Teams of up to 5 ok, pair programming ok, working by yourself also ok (springboard projects great for this - you’ll be able to work alongside other people doing the same stuff) - we will help you find a team if you want one.

What experience do I need?

One of the things about Unhackathon is that it’s a great place jump right into something completely new. Designers, game programmers, web developers and hardware hackers are all more than welcome. Use this opportunity to get outside your comfort zone and learn something new.

What does attending cost?

It’s free! We obviously don’t have exact numbers, but we can say the event itself is free and that we intend to reimburse travel if we can.

What should I bring with me?

Your brain, and your heart! And anything else that you need to be your most productive. Laptop, peripherals, sweatshirt, change of clothes, and a pillow and sleeping bag if you plan on napping. We’re working on getting some hardware to hack on, but you should plan on bringing anything you definitely need.

What is the environment like?

Good food, friends, personal attention, health. view of the city, natural light, community, varied spaces.

What kinds of projects can I work on?

What you'll build is up to you. We will have resources to help you get started and mentors to help you if you can't think of anything to build.
